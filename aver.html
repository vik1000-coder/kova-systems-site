The decision

Is this change safe enough to ship?

Model swaps can lower cost or improve quality, but aggregate scores often hide the exact cases that changed. Aver is designed around the release decision rather than a generic benchmark.

01 / REPLAY

Use your workload

Evaluate current and candidate configurations on prompts or cases that represent the behavior your application actually depends on.

02 / COMPARE

Find the differences

Surface changed outputs, likely regressions, and the exact examples that deserve review—not only a single average score.

03 / DECIDE

Apply a reliability policy

Choose the simplest, lowest-cost tested configuration that meets the standard you defined for the release.

Current product scope

Focused by design.

Aver is currently a model-change assurance prototype. It is not presented as a universal certification system or a complete LLM operations platform.

What Aver is designed to do

  • Compare a current and candidate model or configuration
  • Run a defined set of real or representative cases
  • Expose the cases where behavior changed
  • Support a workload-specific pass or review policy
  • Preserve a record of inputs, settings, and results

What is not yet a managed claim

  • Full end-to-end agent or application certification
  • General RAG, tool-use, and multi-turn effectiveness testing
  • A guarantee that an AI system will never fail in production
  • Regulatory approval or independent legal compliance
  • Replacement for engineering and domain-expert review
Test the change. Inspect the failures. Ship only what meets the policy.

Aver’s intended operating loop

For individual builders

Check whether a cheaper or newer model preserves the behavior your product relies on before changing production.

For product teams

Create a shared review artifact for product, engineering, and domain stakeholders rather than debating a dashboard average.

For governed releases

Retain tamper-evident release evidence as a supporting trust layer—not as a substitute for the evaluation itself.

Have a real model change to test?

Early pilots should begin with one application, one candidate change, and a clearly defined reliability target.
