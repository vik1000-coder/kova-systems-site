# Kova Systems launch checklist

## Entity and public identity

- [ ] Massachusetts Certificate of Organization approved.
- [ ] Exact legal name copied from the approved filing.
- [ ] Registered agent and public addresses reviewed for privacy and accuracy.
- [ ] EIN obtained and banking/accounting separation established.
- [ ] Annual report and tax calendar recorded.
- [ ] Any required business certificates / DBAs for Aver or PrePIEP reviewed.
- [ ] Domain and product-name conflicts checked.

## Website configuration

- [ ] `entityFormed` switched only after approval.
- [ ] Controlled domain and email addresses inserted.
- [ ] Sitemap and robots domain replaced.
- [ ] Social preview image added if desired.
- [ ] Hosting environment forces HTTPS.
- [ ] Security headers verified after deployment.
- [ ] 404 routing tested.
- [ ] Mobile, keyboard, screen-reader, and reduced-motion behavior checked.

## Privacy and legal

- [ ] Terms and Privacy Notice reviewed against actual operations.
- [ ] Hosting, email, analytics, support, form, and error-tracking vendors listed where appropriate.
- [ ] Data-retention schedule documented.
- [ ] Process for privacy requests and incident response documented.
- [ ] No unsupported compliance claims published.
- [ ] Insurance needs reviewed before handling higher-risk information or giving paid outputs.

## Aver before pilots

- [ ] Pilot statement of work defines the exact model/configuration change.
- [ ] Evaluation cases, metrics, reviewer roles, and pass policy are documented.
- [ ] Customer confirms it has rights to provide prompts, outputs, and data.
- [ ] Data use, retention, deletion, confidentiality, and subprocessors are specified.
- [ ] Output language avoids guarantees and states evaluation limits.
- [ ] Security and support contact paths are operational.

## PrePIEP before document uploads

- [ ] Product-specific privacy notice and terms completed.
- [ ] Adult account and age-gating approach defined.
- [ ] Student-record data map and classification completed.
- [ ] Encryption, access control, logging, deletion, backup, and incident response tested.
- [ ] Vendor contracts and data-processing terms reviewed.
- [ ] Clear non-legal/non-medical/non-diagnostic boundaries implemented in product UX.
- [ ] Human escalation path and harmful-output review defined.
- [ ] Appropriate professional and cyber insurance reviewed.
- [ ] No upload path enabled until every required control is ready.
